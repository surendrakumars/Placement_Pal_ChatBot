**Building a High-Scoring Resume for Entry-Level Software Engineering and Technology Placement Roles**

**Resume Structure**

A well-structured resume is essential to effectively showcase your skills, experience, and achievements to potential employers. The following sections should be included in your resume:

1. **Contact Information**
	* Full name
	* Email address
	* Phone number
	* LinkedIn profile (or other professional social media handles)
	* Personal website or blog (if applicable)
2. **Summary/Objective**
	* A brief overview of your background, skills, and career goals (1-2 sentences)
	* Tailor your summary to the specific job you're applying for
3. **Education**
	* Degree(s) earned (Bachelor's, Master's, Ph.D., etc.)
	* Institution(s) attended
	* Relevant coursework or specializations
	* Date(s) of graduation
4. **Experience**
	* List relevant work experience, internships, or projects in reverse chronological order
	* Use action verbs (see below) to describe your responsibilities and achievements
	* Quantify your achievements using metrics (see below)
5. **Projects**
	* List personal or academic projects that demonstrate your skills and experience
	* Include a brief description of the project, your role, and any notable achievements
6. **Skills**
	* List relevant programming languages, tools, and technologies
	* Include any relevant certifications or licenses

**Action Verbs**

Using action verbs is essential to describe your responsibilities and achievements in a clear and concise manner. Some examples of action verbs include:

* Managed
* Created
* Developed
* Designed
* Implemented
* Improved
* Increased
* Enhanced
* Maintained
* Collaborated

**Quantifying Achievements**

Quantifying your achievements using metrics is crucial to demonstrate the impact of your work. Some examples of metrics include:

* "Improved code quality by 30% through the implementation of automated testing and code review processes."
* "Reduced project timeline by 25% through the optimization of development processes and resource allocation."
* "Increased user engagement by 50% through the development of a new feature and its successful deployment."

**Tips for Writing a High-Scoring Resume**

1. **Tailor your resume to the job**: Customize your resume for each job you apply to by highlighting the skills and experiences that match the job requirements.
2. **Use specific examples**: Use specific examples to demonstrate your skills and experiences, rather than making generic claims.
3. **Use action verbs**: Use action verbs to describe your responsibilities and achievements.
4. **Quantify your achievements**: Use metrics to demonstrate the impact of your work.
5. **Keep it concise**: Keep your resume concise and easy to read by using bullet points and clear headings.
6. **Proofread**: Proofread your resume multiple times to ensure there are no errors in grammar, spelling, or formatting.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**

1. **Generic summaries**: Avoid using generic summaries that don't provide any specific information about your background, skills, or career goals.
2. **Lack of metrics**: Avoid making claims about your achievements without providing any metrics to support them.
3. **Typos and grammatical errors**: Avoid typos and grammatical errors, as they can make a negative impression on the reader.
4. **Irrelevant information**: Avoid including irrelevant information, such as hobbies or personal interests, unless they are directly related to the job.
5. **Overemphasis on education**: Avoid overemphasizing your education, as it's not the only factor that determines your qualifications for a job.
6. **Lack of relevant experience**: Avoid applying for jobs without relevant experience or skills, as it can make it difficult to get hired.

**Additional Tips**

1. **Use a standard font**: Use a standard font, such as Arial, Calibri or Helvetica, to make your resume easy to read.
2. **Use bullet points**: Use bullet points to break up large blocks of text and make your resume easier to read.
3. **Use a clear and concise format**: Use a clear and concise format to make your resume easy to read and understand.
4. **Include relevant certifications**: Include any relevant certifications or licenses to demonstrate your expertise.
5. **Get feedback**: Get feedback from friends, family, or a career counselor to ensure your resume is effective and well-written.
